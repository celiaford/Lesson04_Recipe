It's me, the eggplant you forgot about.

...time to make pizza bites.

[A recipe from Celia's covid burnout kitchen]

Sometimes, that eggplant that seemed like an excellent Imperfect Produce steal at the time ends up haunting you at every meal, cackling from the back corner of the crisper drawer.

You know you need to eat the damn eggplant before it goes bad, but you don't want to eat stupid vegetables for dinner tonight. You want pizza.

I present to you: eggplant pizza bites. A surprisingly delicious loophole for desperate times.

INGREDIENTS

  • That sneaky eggplant (one sizeable one is enough)
  • Cooking oil (any oil is probably fine? I don't know the difference. I use Trader Joe's avocado oil spray)
  • Salt
  • Your favorite Italian-ish herbs
  • A couple handfuls of shredded mozzerella
  • Pizza sauce (traditional red sauce, or whatever you prefer. I'm a vodka sauce fan, personally)
  • Any additional toppings your heart desires (arugula is my favorite)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Cut the eggplant into ~3/4 inch rounds.
  2. Place the slices on a paper towel and throw salt on 'em (don't be shy) - this will draw out some moisture and trick this eggplant into acting more like a pizza crust.
  3. Preheat the oven to 400F.
  4. Read a couple emails, do a little dance around the kitchen, pet your cat.
  5. Brush (or spray) the eggplant slices with oil and sprinkle with herbs.
  6. Pop in the oven and roast for 15 minutes.
  7. Do those dishes that have been piling up in your sink. Read a little more. Pace around the house. Maybe do a few pushups.
  8. Take the slices out of the oven, and crank the knob to "broil" (or 500F).
  9. This is the fun part: spoon a little sauce on each slice. Add a generous sprinkle of cheese. Feeling fancy? Add something else (but don't go too crazy -- try to maintain structural integrity).
  10. Pop back in the oven until the cheese melts (just a couple minutes).
  11. EAT. They're delicious, but not very filling. Add a hearty side salad if this is a meal. Or, if it's 1am and this is a snack, they may stand alone.
